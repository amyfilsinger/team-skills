# ClassDojo Schoolwide Playbook (Source of Truth)

This document is the synthesized set of "ideal" talking points, proof points, and
plays for selling "going schoolwide" with ClassDojo. It is compiled from the
official ClassDojo enablement materials:

- `Schoolwide Two-Pager` — external-facing one-pager
- `ClassDojo Schoolwide Talking Points` — internal messaging framework
- `ClassDojo Going Schoolwide Training` — rep training deck with three
  sales situations and role-play scenarios

Use this doc as the yardstick when comparing what reps are actually saying on
Gong calls versus what the enablement intends.

---

## The Core Thesis

"ClassDojo in a few classrooms is a great teacher tool. ClassDojo in every
classroom transforms how the whole school connects with families."

When every staff member is on ClassDojo:
- Families stop getting scattered updates and start feeling connected to the school
- Culture grows stronger because values are visible in every classroom
- Leaders finally have one place to see and shape the full picture
- Renewal conversations become easier because impact is measurable

---

## The Five Value Pillars

Reps should anchor the schoolwide pitch on these five pillars. A strong call
surfaces at least three of them in context.

### 1. Higher family engagement at scale
- Schools that go schoolwide see **2x the family engagement** vs. partial adoption
- Schools with just 2–4 teachers using ClassDojo see **12+ weekly communications per family**; 16+ teachers pushes that to **20+/week**
- **90%+ of messages are read the same day**
- **Nearly 9 in 10 school leaders** cite "giving every family consistent, daily connection" as their top reason for going schoolwide

### 2. One platform, everything runs from one place
- Teachers aren't switching between tools
- Leaders aren't piecing the picture together from 5 different systems
- Communication, behavior, family engagement, and culture live in one app
- The school just *runs* on it

### 3. You're not asking teachers to learn something new
- Most schools going schoolwide already have teachers and families using ClassDojo
- Going schoolwide "makes what already works the standard for everyone"
- Frame as *formalize and amplify what's already working*, never *rip and replace*

### 4. Equity — every family gets the same experience
- Automatic translation in **190+ languages** — no family left out
- No classroom goes dark
- Consistent, daily connection across every grade and room

### 5. Visibility and culture for leaders
- School Leader Insights show which teachers are active, which classrooms are engaged, where support is needed
- Schoolwide Points reinforce shared values across every classroom
- Stories and moments flow from every classroom to every family
- Culture stops varying room to room and becomes something the whole school shares
- Walk into a board meeting and **show**, not just claim, that every family is reached

## The Three Canonical Sales Situations

Reps should recognize these and adapt their pitch accordingly.

### Situation 1: ClassDojo is one of many comms tools

**Setup:** The school uses ClassDojo, but *also* posts through email, Remind,
Facebook, and paper flyers. Families get messages from 5+ places.

**Discovery questions:**
- How many tools do your teachers use to reach families right now?
- Are families actually reading what gets sent through those channels?
- When there's something urgent, do families know where to look?

**Core problem to unlock:** Families stop checking all the channels. Important
messages get lost. Families disengage, teachers feel unheard, school culture erodes.

**Schoolwide talking points to lean on:**
- Increased family engagement (2x with schoolwide)
- Culture becomes central and visible
- Everything runs from one place
- Reference Hamilton Elementary

### Situation 2: Uneven adoption across the school

**Setup:** Some teachers (usually lower grades) love ClassDojo, but upper grades
are resistant. Leadership is bought in but won't mandate.

**Discovery questions:**
- Which grade levels are using ClassDojo today? Where are the gaps?
- What's holding back the teachers who haven't adopted yet?
- How are families in upper grades hearing from teachers right now?

**Core problem to unlock:** Families get a different experience depending on
the classroom. Non-adopting teachers are solving the same problems with worse
tools. Families disengage in upper grades; leaders lose visibility.

**Schoolwide talking points to lean on:**
- Leaders get the visibility they've been missing
- Families know where to look — one place for every update
- 9 in 10 leaders chose schoolwide for consistent family experience
- Elementary families become your advocates — bring that energy into every grade

### Situation 3: Lack of visibility into real adoption

**Setup:** Leadership says they're "schoolwide" but can't verify every teacher
is actually using the platform to communicate with families.

**Discovery questions:**
- How many of your teachers are actively using ClassDojo right now?
- Do you know which teachers are driving strong family engagement?
- If your superintendent asked for proof of family engagement tomorrow, what would you show them?

**Core problem to unlock:** Great teacher work is happening in pockets that
leadership can't see. Wins can't be celebrated, replicated, or reported.

**Schoolwide talking points to lean on:**
- School Leader Insights — see what's working and help it spread
- "Your teachers are doing the hard work — going schoolwide gives leadership
  the chance to recognize it"
- "You're not starting from scratch — you're scaling what works"
- Make the wins visible

## Expected Objections (and How Enablement Says to Handle Them)

### "We already use [Remind / ParentSquare / Apptegy / Thrillshare]"
**Handle:** Don't argue feature parity — anchor on engagement outcomes.
Schoolwide ClassDojo delivers 2x engagement vs. partial tools. Show Hamilton
Elementary. Frame the existing teacher base as a *head start*, not a conflict.

### "ClassDojo is a classroom tool, not a district platform"
**Handle:** This is the #1 perception barrier. Lean on School Leader Insights,
schoolwide announcements, the reachability dashboard, and board-ready reporting.
Cite 96% of school leaders saying ClassDojo makes communication easier.

### "What about teachers already using ClassDojo for free?"
**Handle:** The district license *enhances* what teachers already love. Data
continuity, no migration pain. "Formalize and amplify what's already working."

### "My IT team won't support another app"
**Handle:** SSO, SIS auto-rostering, COPPA/FERPA/GDPR/SOC 2 compliant, Common
Sense Privacy Seal. Minimal IT lift.

### "Show me secondary school engagement data"
**Handle:** Elementary families become the advocates. Trust built in K–5 carries
into middle/high. Point to the upper-grade uplift when schools go fully
schoolwide.

### "I don't want to mandate it for teachers"
**Handle:** (Rob King scenario) Reframe from "mandatory" to "the connected
standard." Once the infrastructure is schoolwide, teachers opt in naturally
because families expect it. Uneven adoption *creates* the equity problem
leadership is trying to avoid.

## The Cost of Inaction (Use This Frame)

When schools stay at partial adoption:
- ClassDojo stays a "nice-to-have" tool, easily deprioritized at renewal
- Competitors find an easy opening with district leaders
- Families get a different experience in every classroom
- Renewal conversations become harder because impact is invisible

When schools go schoolwide:
- ClassDojo becomes "the way the school runs" — renewals almost sell themselves
- Activation lift (e.g., 50% → 65%) makes up for new-logo gaps
- Stronger school culture helps tell the district-wide story
- Renewal = easier to show value and close

## What a "Good" Schoolwide Conversation Looks Like

When reviewing a call, a strong rep will:

- [ ] Ask at least 2 discovery questions before pitching
- [ ] Name at least 3 of the 5 value pillars
- [ ] Cite at least one proof-point stat (2x engagement, 9/10 leaders, etc.)
- [ ] Reference a proof point account (Hamilton Elementary or similar)
- [ ] Reframe objections using the enablement answers, not generic pushback
- [ ] Frame schoolwide as amplifying existing teacher adoption, not replacing it
- [ ] Tie schoolwide to a specific leader priority (renewal, board, equity, visibility)

A weak rep will:

- [ ] Lead with features, not outcomes
- [ ] Miss the discovery step entirely
- [ ] Use generic "more users = more value" framing without stats
- [ ] Fail to name the cost of *partial* adoption
- [ ] Accept "we're already schoolwide" at face value without probing
- [ ] Miss the equity / consistent-family-experience angle
