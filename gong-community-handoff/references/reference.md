# gong-community-handoff — Reference

## What "deploying an advocate" means
This skill does not recruit new advocates. It identifies the right *type* of mentor or story to surface for a specific district gap, then hands off to AJ to find the match from existing Mentor Program participants or story assets.

## Active community programs and motions

| Program | What it is | How it gets deployed against a gap |
|---|---|---|
| **Mentor Program** | Formal volunteer teacher advocates | Peer intro, reference call, story asset — AJ matches mentor profile to district context |
| **Bettermode community space** | Peer learning forum | Not a deployment play for district leaders — this is teacher-facing |
| **More for Mentors** | Quarterly event series | Can be used as proof of community health in district conversations |
| **Customer stories** | Ad hoc, no formal pipeline yet | Surface existing stories; flag new story opportunities back to AJ |
| **Reference asks** | Informal, routed through CS | Flag to CSM; never ask directly without CS awareness; no public reference without legal sign-off |

## Mentor profile dimensions
When describing an ideal mentor match, use these dimensions:
- Grade level (elementary / middle / secondary)
- Subject or classroom type
- District size (small / mid-size / large)
- Region (if relevant)
- Use case (family engagement / behavior / portfolio / secondary)
- Tech comfort level of their school context
- Language context (bilingual, ESL, etc.)

## Compliance notes
- No public reference without legal sign-off — always flag in output
- Use "District A / B / C" in written briefs when pasting sensitive account names
- Do not share raw Gong transcript text outside internal tools

## Gold example

### Input
**Source:** Gong full transcript (exported via browser)
**Account context:** District A — mid-size CA district, ~5 schools (3 elementary, 1 middle, 1 high), large Latino/Spanish-speaking family population, currently paying ~$30K/yr for ParentSquare, new superintendent just onboarded, leadership team in transition. IT Manager is the ClassDojo point of contact; not the final signer.
**Goal:** `all`

**Redacted transcript snippet (key signal moments):**

> Oz (District A IT Manager), 1:01 — "It's kind of a holding pattern. There's been a lot of changes. We have a new superintendent... I'd say let's kick the ball a little bit."

> Oz, 1:40 — "Budgets aren't getting any better."

> Oz, 5:15 — "Our new soup is really techie... I got to tell him to pump the brakes. We got to make sure these things are safe."

> Oz, 10:35 — "The Latino community, it's so hard to get interaction from them. Do you guys have any visibility on that? Is there like a bilingual? Can you get, can your stuff just be swapped to Spanish?"

> Oz, 16:38 — "Flowery's probably been using it since before she left [referring to incoming associate superintendent]. So, hey, ClassDojo's still being used there? Why don't we make this a thing across the district."

---

### Output

**Executive line:** The strongest deployment opportunity is Oz's explicit doubt about reaching Latino families — a verbatim, specific concern that a bilingual CA district mentor could neutralize directly. Separately, a peer story from a district that switched from ParentSquare and saved money would give Oz the ammunition he needs to make the case to a new, budget-conscious superintendent he hasn't yet brought into the conversation.

**Deployment opportunities (top 2 for this call):**

| Gap / doubt (quote) | Gap type | Ideal mentor or story profile | Why this would land | Suggested ask to AJ | Confidence |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| "The Latino community, it's so hard to get interaction from them. Do you guys have any visibility on that? Is there like a bilingual? Can you get, can your stuff just be swapped to Spanish?" (Oz, 10:35) | Use case doubt | CA elementary mentor, bilingual Spanish/English school context, family engagement use case, experience reaching Latino families; ideally mid-size district similar to District A | Oz isn't doubting ClassDojo broadly — he's doubting it for his specific community. Brayden answered technically; a mentor answers experientially. A teacher from a comparable bilingual CA district saying "here's how parent connection changed for our Spanish-speaking families" is the only thing that closes this gap | Find a bilingual CA Mentor Program match who can speak to Latino family engagement; route as a peer conversation, not a reference call | **High** |
| "Budgets aren't getting any better" (Oz, 1:40) + district paying ~$30K/yr for ParentSquare | Objection pattern | CA or West Coast district leader or admin who switched from ParentSquare to ClassDojo and can speak to the cost savings from a peer (not vendor) perspective | Oz already said out loud "they're already using it, we get the same functionality but for almost 30 grand" — he believes the math. What he needs is a peer who can say the same thing so he can repeat it to the new superintendent with credibility | Surface an existing ParentSquare-to-ClassDojo story or find a mentor who made that switch; brief them on the cost consolidation angle specifically | **High** |

**Watchouts:**
- Oz mentioned wanting something to replace Amira (their reading tool) with teacher-level reading analysis and class-wide reporting. Dojo Sparks is parent-facing and doesn't replicate Amira's teacher/admin reports. **Product feedback** — do not send a mentor to address this; it will backfire if the mentor's experience doesn't match the feature ask.

---

### What made this a good example
- Oz gave two verbatim, speaker-labeled quotes with specific demographic and cost context — exactly the signal quality the skill is designed to extract.
- The Latino family engagement doubt is the kind of gap a sales pitch can't close but a 10-minute peer conversation can.
- The Amira watchout is a good illustration of the "sounds like a community play but isn't" pattern — it's a product gap, not a trust gap.
