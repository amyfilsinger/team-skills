# Dimension checklist

A 12-dimension pre-flight checklist. Before writing the battlecard, answer each "did you cover this?" question. Missing dimensions should be explicitly flagged in the output (as "no signal in lookback window") rather than silently omitted.

Use this as a self-review. If you can't answer yes or "legitimately empty" for every row, keep researching.

## Quick version: the 12 dimensions

| # | Dimension | Minimum signal to pass |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Recent moves | At least checked their /press page + top 10 news results in lookback window |
| 2 | Pricing | At least one sourced price point; or explicit "pricing not public" with evidence |
| 3 | User complaints | Reddit JSON queried + at least one review site checked; themes clustered |
| 4 | Launches / roadmap | Changelog or blog-product category checked; or confirmed no updates |
| 5 | Exec & team changes | LinkedIn company people page checked |
| 6 | Hiring signal | Careers page extracted; at least total count + category breakdown |
| 7 | Funding / corporate | Crunchbase or news searched |
| 8 | Customer wins/losses | Customers page scraped + reverse search ("switched from") |
| 9 | Security / legal | News search done; skip section if nothing confirmed |
| 10 | Partnerships | Integrations page scraped |
| 11 | Marketing / GTM | At least webinar / blog category scan |
| 12 | Mobile / quality | App store ratings pulled (if they have mobile) or status page checked |

---

## Full self-review

For each dimension, the checklist below. Answer every question before writing the battlecard.

### 1. Recent moves

- [ ] Did I check the competitor's own `/press` or `/news` page?
- [ ] Did I run a news search scoped to the lookback window?
- [ ] Did I check at least one industry trade pub?
- [ ] For each move I'm including, do I have a date and a publisher source (not just a content farm)?

### 2. Pricing

- [ ] Did I navigate to the pricing page (or confirm they don't have one)?
- [ ] Did I check at least one indirect source (G2 / Capterra / marketplace)?
- [ ] Did I check Wayback Machine for historical pricing?
- [ ] Are minimums, onboarding fees, and promo expirations captured?
- [ ] Is my tear-down table complete with source URLs?

### 3. User complaints

- [ ] Did I run Reddit JSON search across all of Reddit?
- [ ] Did I check at least 3 vertical-relevant subreddits specifically?
- [ ] Did I pull at least one review page (G2 / Capterra / Software Advice / GetApp)?
- [ ] Did I filter reviews to 1–2 star for complaint signal?
- [ ] Did I cluster complaints into themes with recurrence counts?
- [ ] Do I have at least one **verbatim** quote per theme with source and date?
- [ ] Did I check app store reviews if they have a mobile product?

### 4. Launches / roadmap

- [ ] Did I check their blog for product launches in lookback window?
- [ ] Did I look for a dedicated changelog / release-notes / whats-new page?
- [ ] Did I scan their YouTube channel for recent demos?
- [ ] For each launch, do I have a ship date and source?
- [ ] Did I note our equivalent feature or flag the gap?

### 5. Exec & team changes

- [ ] Did I check LinkedIn company people tab?
- [ ] Did I look for specific role changes (CRO, VP CS, CPO, CEO)?
- [ ] Did I check news for "named" / "appointed" / "departed"?
- [ ] For each change, do I have a date?

### 6. Hiring signal

- [ ] Did I scrape their careers page?
- [ ] Did I also check LinkedIn Jobs for their company?
- [ ] Did I categorize by role type (sales / engineering / product / CS)?
- [ ] Did I identify concentration areas or growth velocity?
- [ ] Did I compare against Wayback to see what roles closed recently?

### 7. Funding / corporate

- [ ] Did I check Crunchbase for recent funding?
- [ ] For public companies: did I check SEC filings?
- [ ] For private: did I check news for announcements?
- [ ] If an acquisition happened, did I research the acquiree?

### 8. Customer wins/losses

- [ ] Did I scrape their /customers and /case-studies pages?
- [ ] Did I do a reverse search for customers leaving them?
- [ ] Did I check school-board / council meeting minutes for vendor changes (if applicable to vertical)?
- [ ] Did I surface their customer wins that matter in our geography / segment?

### 9. Security / legal / regulatory

- [ ] Did I check news for lawsuits / breaches / incidents?
- [ ] Am I only including confirmed, sourced items?
- [ ] If nothing confirmed, am I explicitly skipping this section?

### 10. Partnerships & integrations

- [ ] Did I scrape their /integrations page?
- [ ] Did I check relevant partner marketplaces (AppExchange, Clever, etc.)?
- [ ] Did I identify ecosystem gaps (things we integrate with, they don't)?

### 11. Marketing / GTM

- [ ] Did I scan their webinar archive for themes?
- [ ] Did I check LinkedIn company posts in the lookback window?
- [ ] Did I look for new category positioning or persona targeting?

### 12. Mobile / product quality

- [ ] Did I check App Store and Google Play reviews (if mobile product exists)?
- [ ] Did I check status page for recent outages?
- [ ] Are rating trends captured (is it going up or down)?

---

## Signal quality bar

For each claim in the battlecard, verify:

- [ ] Has a source URL
- [ ] Has a date (when the source was published, not when we accessed it)
- [ ] Confidence labeled: `[confirmed]`, `[single-source]`, or `[rumor]`
- [ ] Is specific enough for a rep to say on a call without having to explain
- [ ] Is recent enough to be relevant (within lookback window, or explicitly flagged as historical)

Any claim that fails any of these goes in the "Further research needed" section of the battlecard, not the main sections.

---

## Completeness vs. usefulness

Comprehensive ≠ useful. If every dimension has been researched but the talking points are weak, you've failed. The dimension checklist is a floor for research coverage — not a ceiling for battlecard content.

**Rule**: every dimension researched. Not every dimension included. Sections with thin or no signal get flagged briefly (one line) and dropped from the main flow.
